Caesar Cipher

The Caesar cipher, named after Julius Caesar, is the oldest type of cipher. It is a shift cipher, which means it moves along the alphabet according to it’s key. According to Roman historian Suetonius, in his 'found in Life of Julius Caesar 56', Caesar used this cipher with a key shift of 3 when he needed to make any messages a secret.

An example of how this works is if we take the letter A and we use a shift key of 3 our original letter will be changed to D. Then B would become E and so on.

A physical example of a Caesar cipher, which has been used throughout time as a children's toy, is shown on the left.

The issue with this type of cipher is that once detected as a Ceasar cipher it can easily be decrypted by manueving the alphabet until the setence makes sense. A Caesar cipher is a well known type of cipher which is easily decoded, making this a poor choice of encrypting.
