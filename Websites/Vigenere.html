Vigeneré Cipher

The Vigeneré cipher was originally described in 1553 by Giovan Battista Bellaso. However, in 1586 it was named after Blaise de Vigenére after he published his autokey cipher. The Vigeneré cipher, which is similar to the Caesar cipher, uses a series of linked alphabets to encode the message.
This type of cipher is known as a Polyalphabetical Substitution.

To use this cipher you have to match the letter with the key. For example, take the word 'Write', seen below, with the key 'abc'.

First you need to write out the key so it's long enough to match the 5 letter word 'Write', to do this you rewrite the key multiple times until it matches the length.

Then match each letter up to the corrosponding letter inside the table.

Input: W R I T E
Key: A B C A B
Output: W S K T F

This shows the results of matching W with A and so on.
