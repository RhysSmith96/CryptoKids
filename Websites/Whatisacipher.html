What is Cryptography?


Cryptography is defined as the art of writing or solving codes . The word cryptography is derived from Greek-kryptos which translates to hidden.

In order to write or solve a code, a cipher can be used.   

A cipher is a mathematical way of rearranging letters or substituting them to perform an encryption or decrpytion. Algorithms, which is a process or a set of rules to be followed like a list of instructions, change how the users input text results in a ciphered code.

A code is a mixture of symbols, letters or even words that replace sentences or messages to send or store information. It's a method of shortening messages or keeping them secret.

The reason we use ciphers is to be able to disguise secretive information when communicating with another person incase the message is intercepted. There are multiple different styles of ciphers and methods of encrypting a message.
